About Plein Air

If you are new to plein air painting, you may have a lot of questions. Let’s start with the basics.

What is plein air painting? En plein air is French for “the open air” or “outdoors.” So as the name implies, plein air painting is taking your painting gear and painting outdoors. As you may have guessed by the name, it is attributed to a French painter named Pierre-Henri de Valenciennes (1750-1819). While it was not a new practice then, it was made more feasible with the invention of tubed paint and portable box easels. Today, the gear has evolved to make plein air painting accessible to painters of all skill levels, mediums, and budgets.
